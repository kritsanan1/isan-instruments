## ğŸ¤ Ethical Data Collection Protocol

### **Core Principles**

#### 1. **Cultural Respect & Consent**
- **Primary Rule**: Always obtain explicit consent from performers/communities
- **Attribution**: Properly credit musicians, communities, and cultural contexts
- **Benefit Sharing**: Ensure communities benefit from research/commercial use
- **Cultural Context**: Understand the cultural significance before collection

#### 2. **Academic Collaboration**
- **Partner with Universities**: UW, Cornell, SOAS have Southeast Asian music programs
- **Ethics Review**: Submit to Institutional Review Board (IRB) if academic
- **Cultural Experts**: Consult with ethnomusicologists and cultural anthropologists

#### 3. **Community Engagement**
- **Local Partnerships**: Work with Thai/Isan cultural organizations
- **Reciprocal Benefits**: Offer skills training, equipment, or financial support
- **Knowledge Exchange**: Learn about cultural protocols and traditions

### **Step-by-Step Ethical Collection Process**

#### Phase 1: Research & Preparation (2-4 weeks)
1. **Literature Review**: Study existing research on Isan music
2. **Expert Consultation**: Contact ethnomusicology departments
3. **Cultural Training**: Learn about Thai/Isan cultural protocols
4. **Institutional Support**: Secure academic or institutional backing

#### Phase 2: Community Outreach (4-8 weeks)
1. **Identify Communities**: Contact Isan cultural centers, temples, music schools
2. **Build Relationships**: Attend cultural events, meet musicians
3. **Explain Project**: Clearly communicate research goals and benefits
4. **Obtain Permissions**: Get community leader approval

#### Phase 3: Consent & Collection (2-6 weeks)
1. **Individual Consent**: Detailed consent forms in Thai/Isan languages
2. **Cultural Protocols**: Follow traditional ceremonies if required
3. **Quality Recording**: Use professional equipment in appropriate settings
4. **Documentation**: Record cultural context, playing techniques, stories

#### Phase 4: Data Management & Sharing (Ongoing)
1. **Secure Storage**: Protect sensitive cultural information
2. **Community Access**: Provide copies to communities
3. **Attribution**: Maintain detailed metadata with proper credits
4. **Benefits**: Share research results and potential commercial benefits
